# Pill Buddy — Strip variant

**Project / product family:** Pill Buddy
**Variant:** **Strip** (working name) — retrofit / low-BOM alternate path
**Date:** 2026-05-19
**Status:** Concept sketch. Companion to the Disc / Pocket spec ([`2026-05-15-design.md`](2026-05-15-design.md)).
**Audience:** Same as parent spec — productizable, accessibility-first, no firmware-flashing.

---

## 1. Concept

A retrofit of the **standard drugstore 7-bay weekly pillbox** — the long rectangular tray of seven translucent flip-top compartments labeled S M T W T F S. The mechanical tray itself is unchanged (off-the-shelf or lightly customized injection moulding); all "smarts" live in two add-on pieces:

1. **LED strip** — an addressable RGB strip bonded under the translucent floor of the tray, one (or several) LED per bay. The translucent lid + walls glow with the bay's status color: off / upcoming / due / overdue / taken.
2. **End-cap module** — a small clip-on housing at one short end of the tray, containing the MCU, a small OLED (date, time, today's dose summary, currently-due pill), a confirm button, RTC, battery, and USB-C.

The user reads the strip ambiently ("which bay is glowing red?") and reads the OLED for detail. When they take their pills, they press the button on the end-cap. The bay's LED turns off (or goes to a "taken" color) and the next bay's countdown resumes.

### Why this exists alongside Disc and Pocket

- **Cost** — no rotation mechanism, no camera, no donut, no NFC. BOM is a small fraction of the Disc's.
- **Familiar form factor** — users already own and trust this tray; the retrofit doesn't ask them to learn a new physical object.
- **Bring-your-own-tray potential** — if the end-cap clips to standard trays mechanically, the strip + cap could be sold as an upgrade kit. (Caveat: tray geometry varies; see §7.)
- **Differentiator gap** — without a camera, this variant cannot verify *what was taken*. It only knows *the button was pressed* (Tier 0) or *the lid was opened* (Tier 1). That gap defines the SKU positioning vs Disc.

### Primary user

Adult on 1–4 medications, 1–2 doses/day, who wants reminders + adherence logging but does not need (or want to pay for) pill recognition. Already uses a weekly pillbox.

## 2. Mechanical design

### Tray

- Standard 7-bay weekly pillbox geometry: ~180 × 35 × 25 mm, seven hinged flip-top lids in translucent PP, day-of-week embossed on each lid.
- v1: source an OEM tray and brand it. v1.x: tool a custom tray with an integrated channel under the floor that the LED strip drops into, plus a docking lip at one end for the end-cap.
- Floor is translucent (frosted) so the LED light diffuses through the bay walls + lid evenly rather than appearing as point sources.

### LED strip

- Bonded to the underside of the tray floor with thermal-conductive adhesive.
- v1 baseline: **WS2812B-compatible addressable strip, 1 LED per bay = 7 LEDs total.** Wired to the end-cap via a short ribbon that runs in a moulded channel along the tray's long edge.
- v1.x option: 2-3 LEDs per bay for richer animations (chase, fade) — cost is small, decide after PoC.
- Diffuser: a thin frosted PET film between strip and tray floor smooths hot spots.

### End-cap module

- Clips to one short end of the tray. Mechanical attachment: spring-loaded grips on the tray's end wall, or a magnetic dock with alignment pins (decide in PoC-S2).
- Outer dimensions: ~35 × 35 × 18 mm (matches tray cross-section so the assembly looks like a continuous bar).
- Faces:
  - **Top:** OLED window + single hardware confirm button (large, ~10 mm, ~3 N).
  - **End:** USB-C port for charging.
  - **Tray-facing side:** electrical contacts (pogo or board-to-board) that mate with the LED strip ribbon, plus mechanical clip features.
- Removable for charging, travel, or moving to a second tray (e.g., AM tray vs PM tray).

### Materials

- Tray: food-grade PP (standard).
- End-cap housing: PC/ABS, colour to match parent product family.
- LED strip carrier: flex PCB with conformal coating; sealed under the tray with a peel-and-stick adhesive layer.

## 3. Electronics architecture

Far simpler than the Disc.

### MCU: ESP32-C3 or ESP32-S3-MINI

- ESP32-C3 is enough for this variant (no camera, no on-device ML). Cheaper, smaller package, lower idle current than the S3.
- WiFi + BLE 5.0 for phone pairing + OTA.
- If the platform team prefers a single MCU across SKUs for firmware reuse, use **ESP32-S3-MINI** here and pay the small BOM premium. **Default to the platform-shared part** unless cost gate forces the swap.

### Timekeeping: DS3231SN RTC

- Same part as the Disc. Battery-backed; wakes MCU on dose alarms.

### LEDs

- 7× WS2812B (or 14–21× for multi-LED-per-bay). Driven directly from a single MCU GPIO; no LED coprocessor needed at this scale (the MCU only animates while awake for a reminder).

### Display

- **0.96″ or 1.3″ OLED, 128×64, SSD1306/SH1106, I²C.** Mono is enough for date/time/dose readouts and matches the cost positioning.
- Wakes on RTC alarm, button press, or capacitive tap on the end-cap top surface; sleeps after 20 s idle.

### User input

- **Confirm button** (hardware, 10 mm tactile, audible click).
- Optional **capacitive zone** on the end-cap top for wake-on-tap (ESP32 internal touch peripheral).

### Lid-state sensing (Tier-dependent — see §4)

- **Tier 0 — none.** Button-only confirmation.
- **Tier 1 — per-bay reed switch or Hall sensor.** Tiny magnet glued to each lid; sensor on the LED strip board under each bay. 7 GPIO or a small I²C IO-expander (e.g., PCA9555).
- **Tier 1 alternative — phototransistor per bay.** Detects the light change when a translucent lid opens. Cheaper than reeds, no magnets, but daylight-sensitive; needs ambient-light calibration. Worth a PoC to see if it's good enough.

### Power

- Single 250–400 mAh Li-Po inside the end-cap.
- USB-C charging; standard linear or 1-cell buck charger IC.
- Target battery life: **≥30 days** between charges at default schedule (1–2 doses/day, LEDs idle most of the time, brief reminder animations). Generous margin because the work load is low and the MCU spends most of its life in deep sleep.

## 4. Verification tiers

This variant explicitly does **not** verify pill identity. It can verify, in order of cost:

| Tier | Signal                                          | What we know                                            | BOM impact      |
| ---- | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- | --------------- |
| 0    | Button press at end-cap                         | "User said they took it" — no bay-level evidence        | None            |
| 1a   | Per-bay reed/Hall switch (magnet on each lid)   | "The correct bay was opened around dose time"           | 7× sensor + magnet |
| 1b   | Per-bay phototransistor                         | "The correct bay was opened" — pending PoC ambient-light robustness | 7× photo-trans |
| 2    | Per-bay load cell or capacitive presence        | "The bay was emptied (or content changed)"              | Significant — likely out of scope for this variant |

**v1 ships Tier 0 + Tier 1a.** Tier 0 is the manual-confirm path (always available; equivalent to Mode 4 on the Disc). Tier 1a auto-detects the lid-open event, cross-references it against the schedule, and shows it as the "presumed taken" event subject to user button confirmation. Tier 1b is a PoC; if it works, it becomes a manufacturing-cost downgrade for v1.x.

This tier ladder is what positions the Strip vs the Disc cleanly:

- Disc: "we saw which pills you took" (camera)
- Strip Tier 1: "we saw which bay you opened" (lid sensor)
- Strip Tier 0 / Pocket no-AI: "we trust you that you took it" (button)

## 5. Firmware behaviour

State machine mirrors the Disc's at a coarse level: DEEP_SLEEP / DISPLAY_ACTIVE / DOSE_EVENT / OTA_UPDATE / CHARGING / ERROR. No REFILL_MODE camera calibration (no camera); refill is just a manual checklist in the app.

### Reminder escalation

Identical color/timing semantics to the Disc (so users moving between variants see consistent behaviour):

| Offset       | Bay LED       | OLED + chime                                              |
| ------------ | ------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| T − 15 min   | green (upcoming) | dark                                                      |
| T            | blue (due)    | wakes, shows "Time for [day]'s pills" + 1 s chime         |
| T + 10 min   | yellow (late) | repeat chime + phone push notification                    |
| T + 30 min   | red (overdue) | priority push + chime every 5 min                         |
| T + 60 min   | red, pulsing  | caregiver push (if cloud opted in)                        |

### Dose flow (Tier 1a)

1. RTC alarm → MCU wakes.
2. Today's bay LED goes blue; OLED wakes, shows pills due.
3. User opens the correct bay's lid → reed switch fires → log "lid opened at HH:MM".
4. User takes pills, closes lid, presses end-cap confirm button → log "confirmed taken at HH:MM".
5. Bay LED → off (or "taken" colour for the rest of the day).
6. If user opens the wrong day's bay: OLED warns ("Today is THU — you opened TUE"), bay LED for the opened bay flashes red briefly; offer button to log anyway (for users who do AM/PM splits across bays).
7. If lid opens with no scheduled dose nearby: log as "irregular access," show on OLED, sync to phone for review.

### Dose flow (Tier 0, button-only)

Same as above without the lid-open evidence. Bay LED indicates which bay to open; user opens, takes pills, presses button. The button is the only state change.

## 6. Phone app

Reuses the Pill Buddy app from the Disc spec. New surfaces:

- **Device type:** the app recognises Strip devices over BLE and shows a simpler schedule editor (no per-pill fingerprint workflow — pills are just names + counts).
- **Refill helper:** "fill bay MON: 2× Lisinopril 10 mg, 1× Metformin 500 mg" walkthrough, no camera capture step.
- **Adherence history:** same view as the Disc, with a "verified by: lid sensor / button only" badge per event so caregivers can see the evidence level.

Multi-device support (already on the Disc roadmap for the Pocket companion case) covers Strip + Disc households unchanged.

## 8. v1 MVP scope

### In scope

- Tray + bonded LED strip + clip-on end-cap
- 7× per-bay status LEDs with the Disc's escalation colour scheme
- OLED display: date, time, today's dose summary, due-pill prompt
- Confirm button + Tier 1a per-bay lid sensors
- BLE pairing with the existing Pill Buddy phone app
- Reminders: LED + chime + OLED + phone push
- Cloud opt-in (caregiver dashboard, shared with Disc)
- OTA firmware updates over WiFi (when paired and on charger)

### Deferred

- Custom-tooled tray (v1 uses an OEM tray + adhesive strip + clip-on cap)
- Tier 1b phototransistor sensing (PoC, then v1.x cost-down)
- Voice prompts
- Multi-tray AM/PM hand-off automation (Strip×2 paired to one schedule)

## 9. PoC ladder

PoCs are prefixed `PoC-S*` to distinguish from the Disc's `PoC-1..6`.

### PoC-S1 — LED diffusion through tray floor

- Bond a WS2812B strip + diffuser under an off-the-shelf 7-bay tray. Verify each bay glows evenly through the translucent lid in indoor and bright daylight.
- **Decision gate:** distinct, unambiguous colour per bay at arm's length in indoor + 1000 lux daylight.

### PoC-S2 — End-cap mechanical fit

- 3D-printed end-cap clipping to 3 candidate OEM tray models. Validate fit tolerance, retention force, electrical contact alignment.
- **Decision gate:** end-cap retains across 100 attach/detach cycles on at least 2 tray models without contact wear.

### PoC-S3 — Lid sensor (Tier 1a vs 1b bake-off)

- Build a single-bay rig with a reed/Hall sensor and a phototransistor side-by-side. Cycle 1000 lid open/close events; log false positives/negatives across ambient light variation.
- **Decision gate:** Tier 1a hits ≥99 % accuracy (expected, sanity check); Tier 1b ≥95 % across realistic light → consider for v1.x.

### PoC-S4 — Firmware skeleton on the platform MCU

- Same scope as Disc PoC-5 but trimmed: state machine, RTC alarm wake, BLE pairing, schedule persistence. No camera or CV path.
- **Decision gate:** wakes reliably on all triggers, BLE pairs cleanly, schedule survives reset.

### PoC-S5 — Battery life

- Run a built-up unit through a simulated month (1–2 reminders/day, lid sensor active, BLE advertising at low duty cycle).
- **Decision gate:** ≥30 days from a 300 mAh pack.

## 10. Risks

| ID  | Risk                                                                          | Mitigation                                                                                       |
| --- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| S-R1 | No pill-identity verification → users overdose / dose-stack and we don't see it | Position clearly as "smart reminder," not adherence-grade; show evidence-level badge in app; recommend Disc for high-risk regimens |
| S-R2 | Tray-vendor variability — clip-on cap doesn't fit all OEM trays               | Source a single OEM partner for v1; design v1.x with custom-tooled tray                          |
| S-R3 | Phototransistor reliability under daylight (Tier 1b)                          | Calibrate ambient baseline; fall back to reed/Hall if PoC-S3 fails                               |
| S-R4 | LED diffusion uneven — bays look mis-coloured                                 | PoC-S1 gate; diffuser film + per-bay LED count tuning                                            |
| S-R5 | End-cap pogo contacts wear or oxidise                                         | Board-to-board connector as backup design; spec gold-plated pogos                                |
| S-R6 | User confusion across SKUs ("why doesn't my Strip recognise pills like the Disc?") | App + packaging clearly states verification level; consistent colour/escalation across SKUs reduces UI dissonance |

## 11. Open questions

- **Custom tray vs OEM tray** for v1 — does the saving on tooling outweigh the supplier-risk + fit-variability cost?
- **End-cap attach method** — magnetic dock vs spring clip vs screw. Pending PoC-S2 cycle results.
- **Single MCU SKU across Pill Buddy products** — does the firmware team want ESP32-S3 everywhere (Strip pays a small BOM premium), or ESP32-C3 on Strip (cheaper, two firmware build targets)?
- **LEDs per bay** — 1 (cheap, sufficient if diffusion is even) vs 2-3 (richer animations, only marginally more cost). Pending PoC-S1.
- **Mid-clip variant** — does the end-cap have to be at the end? A centre-clip design halves the LED ribbon run and the lid-sensor wiring run. Industrial design vs visual asymmetry tradeoff.
- **Tier 1b phototransistor go/no-go** — pending PoC-S3.
- **Pricing slot** — Strip vs Pocket (no-AI) overlap. Are they the same slot in the line-up, or does Strip sit slightly above Pocket because it covers a week?
