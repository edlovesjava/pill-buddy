# Pill Buddy — Design Spec

**Project / product family:** Pill Buddy (working title)
**Device variants in this spec:**
- **Disc** (primary, §1-12) — the 7-bay weekly device
- **Pocket** (alt path, §13) — the single-day pocket-sized companion

**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Status:** Brainstorm output → ready for implementation planning
**Audience:** "Productizable for many," accessibility-first (suitable for older adults), no firmware-flashing or technical setup required by the end user

---

## 1. Concept

A ~6″ (152 mm) diameter portable weekly pill organizer. Seven open-top pie-slice "bays" are arranged in a passive ring (the **donut**) that the user rotates manually around a fixed central electronics **hub**. The bay currently at the 6 o'clock "active position" is the one whose pills can be accessed; access is through a single hinged **door** integrated into the hub. The door's underside carries a top-down camera and illumination LEDs, so the device sees pills before and after each open event and verifies what was taken. A round display, three physical buttons, status LEDs, MCU, RTC, and a swappable battery all live in the hub column.

Reminders escalate from local (LED + chime + display) to phone push to an optional caregiver notification. The device works fully without a cloud account; cloud is opt-in solely to enable caregiver sharing.

**Tagline candidate:** "Your pill buddy" — the AI-assisted recognition is the differentiator, but manual confirmation is always available so the device never depends on it.

### Primary user

Adult on 1–8 medications, 1–4 doses/day, who wants reliable reminders without becoming dependent on a phone. Comfortable with a one-time phone-paired setup. May or may not be tech-savvy; UI must be readable, tactile, and forgiving of mistakes.

### Secondary user (opt-in)

A caregiver (family member, home health worker) who wants visibility into adherence. Receives notifications on missed doses and reads an adherence dashboard. Has read-only access; cannot change the patient's schedule.

## 2. Mechanical design ("the donut sandwich")

The device is three nested parts:

1. **Hub assembly** (permanent, contains all electronics):
   - **Foot**: thin flat plate, ~152 mm (6″) diameter, rubberized non-slip underside, with a circular groove on its top surface that the donut rides in.
   - **Column**: cylindrical, ~50 mm (2″) diameter, ~50 mm tall, rises from the center of the foot. Houses display + 3 buttons + 7-LED status ring + speaker + MCU + RTC + battery + USB-C + NFC reader. Top is the user-facing surface. Tight footprint for the display + buttons — see Open Questions.
   - **Top lid**: 6/7 of a circular arc, attached to the top of the column and extending outward to the foot's outer radius. Snap-fit to the column with two release tabs at the rim. Forms the top half of a channel that captures the donut.
   - **Door**: hinged to the column at the 6 o'clock active position; fills the 1/7 gap left in the top lid. Carries the camera + illumination LEDs on its underside, with a flex cable routing through the hinge to the MCU.
2. **Donut** (passive, swappable):
   - Single injection-molded ring with 7 open-top pie-slice bays, rubberized outer rim for grip, magnetic detents on inner edge for click-stop registration. Optionally carries a NFC/RFID tag for multi-donut identification.
3. **Donut lid** — *eliminated in v6.* The hub's top lid covers 6 bays; the hub door covers the 7th. The donut itself has no top.

The donut sits in the channel formed by the foot's groove (bottom) and the top lid's underside groove (top). It can rotate freely but cannot lift out unless the top lid is released. Releasing the top lid is the refill / donut-swap action.

### Active position and rotation

- The active position is fixed at 6 o'clock on the device. A small pointer molded into the hub column marks it.
- Magnetic detents on the inner edge of the donut mate with detent posts in the column to give 7 click-stop positions, one per bay.
- An **AS5600** (or equivalent) magnetic absolute rotary encoder, with a radial diametric magnet bonded to the donut's inner rim, lets the MCU read which bay is at the active position at any time.
- The device displays "rotate to TUE bay" if the user is on the wrong day; the 7 status LEDs around the top of the column indicate per-bay-position state (off / upcoming / due / overdue / taken).

### Door mechanism

- Hinged to the column at the 6 o'clock side, swings up + outward to expose the active bay.
- A small magnet in the door and a Hall sensor in the column detect open/close (also serves as the camera-capture trigger).
- Flex cable through the hinge carries camera data lines + LED control + door sensor signal.
- Closed position physically clamps the donut at the active wedge — prevents donut rotation while the door is open (safety: no surprise rotation while user's fingers are in the bay).

### Refill / swap workflow

1. User presses the snap-tabs on the top lid; top lid lifts off.
2. Donut is now free; user lifts it out (or fills in place).
3. User fills all 7 bays at once on a table (all bays are open-top).
4. Drops donut back into foot's groove.
5. Snaps top lid back on.
6. Device reads donut NFC tag (if present), loads associated schedule + pill calibration.

### Materials

- **Hub body:** PC/ABS, white or warm-grey for the productizable SKU.
- **Donut:** food-grade ABS or PP, white, with thermoplastic-elastomer rubberized rim co-molded.
- **Foot underside:** TPE / silicone non-slip layer.
- **Bay surfaces in contact with pills:** BPA-free, food-grade, smooth-walled for easy cleaning.

### Approximate dimensions

- Outer diameter: **152 mm (6″)**
- Hub column diameter: **50 mm (2″)**
- Bay radial width: **~51 mm (2″)** — outer radius 76 mm minus inner radius 25 mm
- Overall height (including column): ~50 mm with door closed
- Bay capacity: ~25-30 mL each at ~13 mm bay depth (accommodates ~10-15 typical pills per bay, comfortably covers up to 4 doses/day of multiple pills)
- Weight target: <450 g without battery pack

## 3. Electronics architecture

All electronics live in the hub except the camera + illumination LEDs (in the door, connected via flex through hinge).

### Main MCU: ESP32-S3-WROOM-1

- 8 MB PSRAM (camera buffers, on-device ML), 16 MB flash
- WiFi 802.11 b/g/n + BLE 5.0 built-in
- Native capacitive touch (used for wake-on-tap on the hub face)
- Dedicated I²S camera interface (DVP)
- Decision depends on **PoC-1** outcome (see §9). If on-device CV proves insufficient, add a CV coprocessor (RP2350 or a dedicated NPU module like Himax HX6537 or Synaptics SR110) on the door's flex board next to the camera. **Do not** swap the main MCU off ESP32-S3 — its integrated wireless is too valuable to lose.

### Timekeeping: DS3231SN RTC

- Battery-backed, ±2 ppm precision (survives power-off, swaps)
- Generates the alarm interrupt that wakes the ESP32-S3 from deep sleep at scheduled dose times

### LED coprocessor: ATtiny1616 (or equivalent)

- Owns the 7-LED status ring + 2 illumination LEDs
- ESP32-S3 sends state-transition commands over I²C, then sleeps
- Coprocessor runs animations (breathing, blink, color holds) at sub-mA draw
- Key power-saving move: the main MCU never has to be awake just to animate LEDs

### Camera

- **Primary:** 1× OV5640 (5 MP autofocus) in the door, top-down view of active bay
- **Optional secondary:** a second OV5640 at offset position in the door, used only if PoC-2/3 testing shows pill occlusion is unresolved by single-camera view + agitation
- Connected to the ESP32-S3 over DVP

### Sensors

- **AS5600** magnetic rotary encoder — absolute donut position
- **TLE4906** Hall switch in column at the door hinge — door open/close
- **MFRC522 / PN5180** NFC reader at the foot's top center — donut ID tag

### User input / output

- **Display:** 1.28″ round IPS, 240×240, GC9A01 controller, SPI. Daylight-readable.
- **Buttons:** 3× 12 mm tactile membrane buttons (◀ / OK / ▶), ~3 N actuation force, audible click. Sized for accessibility.
- **Capacitive touch:** ESP32-S3 internal touch peripheral on a pad behind the column's top surface, wakes on tap.
- **Speaker:** small magnetic transducer + class-D amp, ~70 dBA at 1 m.
- **Illumination LEDs:** 2× warm-white in the door, PWM-controlled, brief flash only at capture (avoid heat + power waste).
- **Status LEDs:** 7× WS2812B RGB in the hub column top, one per bay-position.

### Power

- **USB-C** on the column for charging the installed battery pack.
- **Battery pack:** swappable, latched to the foot underside. 18650 cell (3.7 V, ~3500 mAh) with protection PCM. Pack has its own USB-C port for cradle / cable charging.
- **Two packs ship with the device**, plus a charging cradle as the in-box charger.
- **Hot-swap hold-up:** supercap (0.5 F) + small coin cell on the main board keep RTC + RAM alive for ~5 minutes during a pack swap.

## 4. Firmware state machine

### States

| State            | Trigger to enter                          | Power draw (est.) | Notes                                          |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| DEEP_SLEEP       | Idle timeout, or post-event               | ~15 µA            | Default                                        |
| DISPLAY_ACTIVE   | Wake events (below)                       | ~30 mA            | Display + UI active, 20 s idle → DEEP_SLEEP    |
| DOSE_EVENT       | RTC alarm OR door open at active bay      | ~250 mA peak      | Camera capture, CV, log, ~5 s                  |
| REFILL_MODE      | Long-press OK or app trigger              | ~30 mA            | NFC read, app-driven calibration               |
| CHARGING         | USB-C plug detected                       | varies            | Higher tolerance for display-on time           |
| OTA_UPDATE       | App command + USB-C connected             | ~150 mA           | Refuses on battery alone                       |
| ERROR            | Self-test failed, low battery, etc.       | varies            | Display shows actionable error code            |

### Wake triggers from DEEP_SLEEP

- DS3231 RTC alarm INT (scheduled dose)
- AS5600 encoder flux change (donut rotated)
- Hall switch in column (door opened)
- Capacitive touch on hub face
- USB-C plug detection
- BLE advertisement timer (low duty cycle when no recent app activity)

### Dose event flow

1. RTC INT fires → MCU wakes, reads current schedule and which bay is at the active position.
2. If wrong bay: display "Rotate to TUE"; LED for today's bay-position pulses blue; chime; wait for rotation or snooze.
3. Right bay active: display shows dose info (pill names + dose strengths + simple shape sketches), bay LED steady blue, chime once.
4. Wait for door open OR snooze OR phone push acknowledge.
5. On door open (Hall switch fires):
   1. Illumination LEDs flash; camera captures "before" frame
   2. Run on-device CV (Mode 1) → expected pills present?
   3. Log to event queue
6. User takes pills, closes door.
7. On door close (Hall switch fires):
   1. Camera captures "after" frame
   2. Diff before/after → list of removed pills by class
   3. Map to scheduled dose by name + time window → mark dose taken
   4. Log "taken at HH:MM" with confidence score
   5. LED coprocessor updates ring to next state
   6. Phone push (if paired) — "Took AM dose at 8:14"
   7. (If cloud opted in) sync event to cloud
8. Return to DEEP_SLEEP within 20 s.

### Reminder escalation timeline (configurable per user)

For a scheduled dose at time T:

| Offset    | Action                                                              |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| T − 15 min | LED for today's bay = green (upcoming)                              |
| T         | LED = blue + 1 s chime + display wakes "Time for [dose]"            |
| T + 10 min | LED = yellow + 2 s chime + phone push notification                  |
| T + 30 min | LED = red + repeated chime every 5 min + phone push (priority)     |
| T + 60 min | Caregiver notification via cloud (if opted in) + push reminder      |

### Refill flow

1. User long-presses OK or taps "Refill" in app → REFILL_MODE.
2. App walks user through bay-by-bay: "what goes in Mon? Tue?" etc. For new pills, capture phone photo + metadata; app pushes fingerprint to hub.
3. On lid + donut reseat: device reads NFC tag, loads donut's schedule.
4. Brief "scan" sequence: rotate to each bay, take a calibration image, store baseline.
5. Exit REFILL_MODE.

## 5. Pill recognition pipeline

### Capture conditions (controlled)

- Status LEDs dimmed to near-off during capture (white-balance preservation)
- 2× warm-white illumination LEDs flash to a calibrated brightness for ≤200 ms
- 1-2 frames captured at 5 MP, JPEG-encoded to PSRAM

### Inference modes (in fallback order)

1. **Mode 1 — Autonomous on-device.** ESP32-S3 (with PSRAM) runs an INT8-quantized per-user CNN trained at calibration time on the user's specific pill library. Detects + counts known pill classes. Target: ≥90 % top-1 accuracy at ≤500 ms latency on an 8-class problem. **This is the v1 target; PoC-1 decides if it's met.**
2. **Mode 2 — Phone-assist over BLE.** Hub streams the JPEG to the paired phone over BLE; phone runs a larger Core ML / TFLite model. Triggered when on-device confidence < threshold OR when the user is adding a new pill type. Adds 1-3 s latency vs Mode 1.
3. **Mode 3 — Cloud-assist over WiFi.** Hub uploads to a cloud vision API. Reserved for unknown pills with no good local match, or for users who explicitly opt into "always use cloud" premium quality. Adds 2-5 s latency + privacy considerations.
4. **Mode 4 — Manual confirm (CV off).** Display prompts the user to confirm each scheduled pill ("Took Lisinopril 10 mg?"); user presses OK. Always available as the ultimate fallback, including a user-selectable "no AI" mode.

### Calibration

Triggered at refill, per bay, per pill type:

1. App prompts user to lay one of each pill type on a neutral surface.
2. Phone captures one or more clear photos.
3. App extracts color centroid, shape descriptor, size in mm (via reference object).
4. App stores: pill name, dose, schedule, fingerprint.
5. App pushes fingerprint to hub over BLE.

Per-user dictionary capped at ~16 pills initially (scalable with on-device flash + model retraining cadence).

### Diff logic

For a dose event:

- `before = set_of_pills(door_open_frame)`
- `after = set_of_pills(door_close_frame)`
- `removed = before - after`
- Match `removed` against the current scheduled dose (by pill name and time window).
- If removed matches dose → mark dose taken, log event.
- If removed is a subset → log partial-dose-taken, warn if remainder won't be taken in time.
- If removed includes unexpected pills → soft warning ("you took an extra Lisinopril this morning — log it anyway?").
- If after-frame is empty when before had pills + the dose is incomplete → log irregular event, prompt user.

## 6. Phone app

### Stack

- React Native or Flutter (single codebase for iOS + Android)
- Native BLE bindings (CoreBluetooth on iOS, system BLE on Android)
- Local storage (encrypted SQLite) for schedules + events
- Cloud SDK pluggable only when cloud opt-in is on

### First-run flow

1. App discovers nearby hubs via BLE.
2. Display on hub shows a pairing QR code.
3. User scans QR with phone app to complete pairing.
4. App prompts: name device, set timezone, confirm RTC (over-the-wire sync from phone).
5. Skipped by default: cloud account. User can enable later.

### Daily flow

- Push notifications on dose events (due, overdue, taken).
- Calendar view with daily adherence streak.
- Trend charts: 7-day, 30-day, all-time adherence.
- Refill predictions: based on pill counts at last refill + current event log, warn ~3 days before a bay runs out.

### Refill / calibration flow

- "Refill" button → walks bay-by-bay, supports new-pill calibration with phone-camera capture.
- Generates pill fingerprints, pushes to hub.
- Optional: capture calibration photos via the hub's own camera (cleaner reference but limited resolution).

### Settings

- Schedule editor (per-dose: time, pill list, escalation timing)
- Cloud opt-in toggle (off by default)
- Caregiver invite (only visible when cloud is on)
- Notification preferences, sound, do-not-disturb hours
- Battery / firmware info, OTA trigger

## 7. Cloud (opt-in, caregiver tier only)

### Activation

Cloud is **never** auto-enabled. The user explicitly opts in from the app, accepts a clear privacy disclosure, and creates an account. Until then, the device + phone are fully functional with zero cloud data flow.

### What's stored

- Dose events: UTC timestamp, pill names, taken/missed/late, confidence score
- Pill catalog: names, doses, schedules (no images by default — fingerprints stay on phone/device)
- Device telemetry: battery level, firmware version, error events
- **Not stored by default:** raw camera images, pill fingerprints, biometric data

### Caregiver model

- Patient generates a single-use QR invite from their app.
- Caregiver installs the same app, scans QR → linked to patient's adherence stream.
- Caregiver app: read-only adherence view, push notifications on missed doses.
- Patient can revoke a caregiver at any time → caregiver loses access immediately, all caregiver-cached PHI invalidated.

### Privacy + compliance posture (US market)

- HIPAA-aligned: BAA with cloud provider, access audit logs, data minimization, no third-party trackers.
- AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit.
- Region-restricted hosting per market (US, EU).
- Account deletion = full PHI purge per retention policy.

### Business model

- Device: one-time hardware purchase + included accessories (cradle, second battery pack).
- Cloud caregiver tier: optional subscription.
  - Suggested: free for 1 caregiver per patient, paid for additional caregivers + image history + cross-device reporting (assisted-living / B2B angle).

## 9. PoC ladder

Each PoC has a clear pass/fail decision gate.

### PoC-1 — CV feasibility on ESP32-S3

- Bench setup: ESP32-S3 dev board + OV5640 + 3D-printed fixed-bay rig (Ender 3) + bench lighting.
- Dataset: 8 common pill types, hand-collected via the same camera, augmented for rotation and partial occlusion.
- Train INT8-quantized classifier off-device (TensorFlow Lite Micro toolchain), deploy to ESP32-S3.
- **Decision gate:** ≥90 % top-1 accuracy at ≤500 ms inference time on an 8-class detect+count problem, with realistic occlusion.
- **Pass:** Mode 1 is the v1 default. Continue with ESP32-S3 main MCU alone.
- **Fail:** add CV coprocessor (RP2350 or NPU module) on the door's flex board, OR fall back to Mode 2 (phone-assist) as v1 primary. Re-cost the BOM.

### PoC-2 — Mechanical prototyping (Ender 3)

- Print donut, foot, column, top lid, door, snap tabs.
- Assemble; validate: rotation feel, detent click force, door swing, snap-release tab geometry, donut-channel clearance, NFC tag clearance.
- Cycle test: 1000 rotations, 500 door open/close cycles, 200 lid release/reseat cycles.
- **Decision gate:** rotation feel is satisfying (subjective panel test), no binding/jamming, all sensors trigger reliably across cycle tests.

### PoC-3 — Door subassembly

- Real flex hinge (not 3D-printed) with camera + illumination LEDs mounted on a small flex PCB in the door, paired with the Hall sensor and door magnet on the column side.
- Test flex cable bend life (target: ≥100,000 cycles before failure).
- Validate camera focus + lighting consistency through the hinge motion range.
- Validate Hall switch debouncing and timing across the door's open/close arc.
- **Decision gate:** ≥100k cycle flex life, capture quality stable across cycle count.

### PoC-4 — Status ring + LED coprocessor

- 7× WS2812B + ATtiny coprocessor + I²C interface to a host MCU.
- Validate animation patterns, power draw at idle + during patterns, transition latency.
- **Decision gate:** sub-mA average draw at idle, smooth animations, <50 ms state transitions.

### PoC-5 — Firmware skeleton

- ESP32-S3 firmware with: state machine, RTC alarm wake from deep sleep, BLE pairing, schedule persistence in flash, dummy event log.
- **Decision gate:** wakes reliably on all triggers, BLE pairs cleanly with a test app, schedule survives reset.

### PoC-6 — Phone app skeleton

- BLE pairing + schedule editor + push notifications, both iOS and Android.
- **Decision gate:** end-to-end "set a schedule, get a notification when it fires" loop works.

### Integration milestone

- All PoCs combined into a working alpha unit (PCB, full mechanicals, real firmware + app).
- Beta user pool: 10 friendly users for 4 weeks; instrument heavily.

## 11. Open questions

These are deliberately unresolved in the spec — they wait on PoC results, market input, or engineering trade-offs:

- **MCU final decision** — ESP32-S3 alone vs +CV coprocessor; pending PoC-1.
- **Camera count** — 1 vs 2 cameras in the door; pending PoC-2/3 occlusion testing.
- **Battery capacity sizing** — pending real-world power draw measurements from PoC-4/5.
- **Donut ID mechanism** — NFC (preferred) vs pogo contacts (backup); pending PoC-2 EMI test.
- **Imprint OCR for v2** — aspirational; pending market signal and regulatory consultation.
- **Pricing target** — entry vs premium SKU split; pending COGS estimate post-PoC-3.
- **Regulatory strategy** — consumer device vs medical device class; pending legal review.
- **Default escalation timing** — current values are guesses; calibrate in beta.
- **Display tech** — IPS (specced) vs OLED (better contrast, more power-hungry); pending PoC-4 viewing-angle test.
- **Button placement geometry on 50 mm column top** — a 32 mm (1.28″) round display + three 12 mm buttons + 7-LED status ring is tight. Options: bump display to 38 mm (1.5″), arrange buttons in an arc around the display rather than below it, or grow the column to ~57 mm. Pending PoC-2 industrial-design pass.

## 13. Alternate path: Pocket (single-day variant)

A companion product / alternate path on the same platform: a single-bay pocket-sized device under the Pill Buddy umbrella. **The hub sits atop a single pill cup** — same hub form factor as the Disc but the camera (when present) points **down** into the cup rather than down into a rotating bay. No donut, no rotation, no 7-way mechanism. Pocket-sized.

### Why this is interesting

- **Lower price point** — far simpler BOM. Even *without* the camera and recognition, it's still a useful "smart pill case" with reminders and tap-to-confirm. Sold as an entry SKU.
- **Natural travel / daily-carry companion** — owners of the Disc fill the Pocket each morning with the day's doses; carry the Pocket and leave the Disc at home.
- **Same platform** — reuses MCU, firmware base, BLE pairing, phone app, schedule model, recognition pipeline. Code reuse is high.
- **AI-cap upsell** — sell two SKUs: "Pocket" (no camera, reminders only) and "Pocket AI" (with camera + recognition).

### What changes from the Disc

- **Single bay** in a transparent or opaque pill cup that screws/clips onto the underside of the hub
- **Camera (optional, AI SKU only)** in the hub, pointing **down** into the cup; same illumination LEDs
- **Smaller display + buttons** — the Disc has a tight 2″ column; the Pocket can be even more compact (e.g., a 1″ display + a single capacitive zone + a hardware confirm button)
- **Smaller battery** — pocket form factor; charged via USB-C cable rather than swappable cradle; ~5-7 day battery life acceptable
- **No status LED ring** (or a single multicolor status LED) — pocket device, doesn't need at-a-glance ambient status
- **No NFC donut ID** — only one cup, no swap
- **Schedule:** Disc owners' Pocket syncs to the Disc's schedule via the app (just the day's relevant doses pushed daily); standalone Pocket users edit schedule directly in the app

### Mechanical sketch

- **Hub:** ~45 mm diameter, ~25 mm tall (round, palm-sized)
- **Cup:** ~45 mm diameter, ~30 mm deep (twist-lock or magnetic to hub)
- **Cup material:** food-grade PP, optionally translucent so user can visually inspect
- **Total assembled:** ~45 mm diameter × ~55 mm tall — fits comfortably in a pocket or bag

### Use cases

1. **Solo (no Disc):** simpler regimens (1-2 daily doses), pocket-only product, entry price point
2. **Disc + Pocket companion:** Disc holds the week, Pocket holds today; refill Pocket each morning from the Disc
3. **AM/PM split:** two Pockets — one for AM, one for PM — handed off as the day progresses
4. **Travel:** Pocket in suitcase, no need to bring the full Disc

### Recognition pipeline notes

- Camera-down into a cup is a **cleaner CV problem than camera-radial into a bay** — no occlusion from container walls, pills can be agitated by gentle shaking before capture, the cup interior is a controlled background.
- If on-device CV fails for the Disc (PoC-1), it may still **pass for the Pocket** because the cup geometry is friendlier. The Pocket could ship Mode 1 (autonomous) even if the Disc has to fall back to Mode 2 (phone-assist) — useful product differentiator.

### Decision

Listed as a **product-family alternate path**, not a v1 commitment. The Disc spec stands as the primary product. A separate Pocket spec to be drafted if/when the team commits to a multi-SKU strategy. Worth keeping in mind during platform decisions (firmware modularity, app multi-device support, account model) so the Disc work doesn't paint the Pocket into a corner.

## 14. Glossary

- **Hub** — fixed central electronics assembly (foot + column + top lid + door).
- **Donut** — passive 7-bay ring that rotates around the hub column.
- **Active position** — the 6 o'clock slot where the door is; only this bay can be accessed.
- **Top lid** — 6/7 arc of plastic attached to the hub column, covers 6 bays.
- **Door** — hinged 1/7 wedge of the hub at the active position; carries camera + illumination LEDs.
- **Donut sandwich** — operating state where the donut is captured between the foot's groove and the top lid's underside.
- **Imprint OCR** — reading the stamped letter/number code on pharmacy pills to identify them via FDA database lookup. Out of scope for v1.
- **Mode 1 / Mode 2 / Mode 3 / Mode 4** — CV inference paths: autonomous on-device, phone-assist over BLE, cloud-assist over WiFi, manual confirm.
- **PoC** — proof-of-concept; gated milestones on the build path.
- **Pill Buddy** — project / product-family umbrella (working title).
- **Disc** — working name for the 7-day weekly device (primary product, §1-12).
- **Pocket** — working name for the single-day pocket variant (§13).
